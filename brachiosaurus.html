Meet the towering Brachiosaurus, a massive herbivore that roamed the Earth during the Jurassic period.

Overview

The Brachiosaurus is a massive herbivorous dinosaur that lived during the late Jurassic period, about 154 to 153 million years ago. It was one of the largest dinosaurs, reaching up to 80 feet long and towering 40 feet tall. Its long neck allowed it to feed on the treetops, making it one of the most distinctive dinosaurs of its time.

Physical Characteristics

Brachiosaurus had a long neck, which it used to reach vegetation high in the trees, unlike many other dinosaurs that had shorter necks. Its front legs were longer than its hind legs, giving it an upright stance. It weighed up to 62 tons and had a relatively small head compared to its massive body.

In Jurassic Park

In *Jurassic Park* (1993), the Brachiosaurus is one of the first dinosaurs seen by the characters as they enter the park. Its towering presence and gentle nature make it one of the most iconic and memorable dinosaurs in the franchise.
