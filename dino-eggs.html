The Mystery of Dino Reproduction

In the Jurassic Park films, a critical piece of the dinosaur cloning process involves the use of dinosaur eggs. Since the cloned dinosaurs were not naturally hatched by their biological mothers, scientists needed to find alternative methods to incubate these creatures. This challenge has inspired discussions and research in both fictional and real-world contexts about how we could potentially grow extinct species in artificial environments.

In reality, dinosaur reproduction, like that of modern birds (their closest living relatives), would have involved eggs. However, no one has yet discovered a viable method to hatch a dinosaur from an ancient egg. In the world of Jurassic Park, the solution was the use of surrogate mothers and artificial incubation methods. But what if we could bypass the need for actual dinosaur eggs altogether? This brings us to the topic of artificial wombs and synthetic incubation.
