Overview

Velociraptors were small but deadly carnivores that roamed the Earth during the late Cretaceous period, approximately 75 to 71 million years ago. Unlike the giant T. rex, these creatures were smaller but were known for their speed, intelligence, and ability to hunt in packs.

Physical Characteristics

Velociraptors were around 6.5 feet long and stood about 3 feet tall. They weighed around 15-33 pounds and were covered in feathers. They had a large sickle-shaped claw on each foot, which they likely used to slash at their prey.

In Jurassic Park

In the Jurassic Park films, Velociraptors are portrayed as highly intelligent predators capable of strategic hunting and teamwork. They are also famous for their ability to open doors, which creates tense moments in the movies.

The Velociraptors' terrifying appearance and strategic behavior made them stand out as one of the most iconic dinosaurs in the franchise.
